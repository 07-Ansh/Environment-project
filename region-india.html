Water Availability and Challenges in India

Punjab

Main Challenges: Significant groundwater depletion due to intensive agriculture, especially rice production.

Primary Water Sources: Groundwater and canal systems fed by rivers.

Water Usage: Primarily agricultural, with a focus on high-yield crops.

Maharashtra

Main Challenges: Droughts, water scarcity in rural areas, and high agricultural water demand.

Primary Water Sources: Rivers, dams, and reservoirs.

Water Usage: Predominantly agricultural, with increasing demand in urban areas.

Gujarat

Main Challenges: Severe water scarcity in arid regions and reliance on Narmada River water.

Primary Water Sources: Rivers, groundwater, and desalination plants along the coast.

Water Usage: High demand for both agriculture and industrial use in urban areas.
